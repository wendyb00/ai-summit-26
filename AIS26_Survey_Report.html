The current sales page has strong bones — the 5-step sequential method and "a method, not a menu" framing are genuinely good. But it's selling to the wrong emotional state. Survey data points to five specific changes that would meaningfully lift conversion.
Priority changes — ranked by impact
1
Kill the 0.1% status hook. Lead with relief.
The current hero sells prestige. Survey data shows the dominant emotional driver is autonomy and relief — "I don't need to rely on anyone else for tech anymore." Status hooks create FOMO. Relief hooks create genuine desire. Proposed swap:

"Be in the top 0.1% of Founders, CEOs & Executives Using AI"
→ "Your Fridays aren't going to free themselves."
Subhead: A 16-week program to build the AI system that runs your business — so you can stop running it into the ground.
2
Move Eliza/BlinkLife from bonus to hero proof point
The most-cited summit highlight across 527 responses is buried at the bottom as a bonus. It should be the axis of the pitch. Lead with the story: "Vishen built an AI that knows his patterns, his voice, his priorities. It calls out his procrastination. It runs his inbox. You build yours in this program." That's the actual product promise — and it's the most emotionally resonant thing you own.
3
Add a summit-aware bridge section at the top
Most visitors arrive from the summit already convinced. The current page re-pitches from scratch as if they're cold leads. Add a 2–3 line bridge: "You've seen the method. You've seen Eliza. You know this works. This is where you build it — your Brain, your Team, your System — with 16 weeks of live coaching and the exact tools Vishen uses daily."
4
Rewrite curriculum headers using survey-validated language
Current headers are functional labels. Survey feedback gives you the real language — use it in the headers.
Current headerRewrite (survey-validated)
Foundations of Applied AI for Work & LifeStop getting generic output. Build an AI that actually knows you.
Create at Scale Without a TeamShip a week's worth of content in a morning. In your voice.
Build Your AI TeamStop being the bottleneck in your own business.

5
Fix the VIP replay promise — it's creating broken expectations
Survey: "I put the weekend aside and purchased VIP access to watch recordings... nothing was posted. Frustrating." The current "30 days post-summit" framing is vague and generating trust damage. Change to a specific promise: "Replay of each session available within 24 hours." Also lead the VIP upsell with Q&A access (rated 4.44–4.46) rather than recordings — that's the stronger emotional hook.
